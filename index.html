The Bhagavad Gita

The Bhagavad Gita ("The Song of God"), often referred to as the Gita, is a 700-verse Hindu scripture that is part of the epic Mahabharata. It is narrated by the sage Veda Vyasa.

It takes place on the battlefield of Kurukshetra, serving as a dialogue between Prince Arjuna and his guide and charioteer, Lord Krishna.
